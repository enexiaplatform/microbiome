## 3. Pinning Cadence Plan (First 4 Weeks)

**Baseline volume:** 3–5 pins per day, spread across morning and evening US time (Pinterest's core audience window). Consistency matters more than spikes — pick a daily number you can sustain and hold it.

**Fresh vs repin ratio:** roughly 80% fresh pins / 20% repins (repin = saving your own existing pin to a second relevant board, or repinning high-quality third-party content in the niche). Pinterest's current guidance favors fresh images; avoid re-pinning the same image-link pair repeatedly within a few days.

| Week | Focus | Daily mix |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Week 1 | Seed all 5 boards with 3–4 launch pins each so no board looks empty | 2 fresh launch pins + 1–2 third-party repins per day |
| Week 2 | Publish the 18-pin launch set at ~2–3 fresh pins/day; start interleaving boards | 2–3 fresh + 1–2 repins |
| Week 3 | Second image variants for the strongest topics (odor returns, bedding, probiotic vs enzyme); new image + same link counts as fresh | 3 fresh + 1–2 repins |
| Week 4 | Review early analytics (impressions, saves, outbound clicks); make fresh variants of the top 5 pins; retire weakest creative | 3–4 fresh + 1 repin |

**Notes:**
- One article can support many pins over time — vary the image and text overlay, keep the link.
- Spread pins for the same article across different days and boards rather than batching them.
- Seasonal hooks (spring cleaning, holiday guests + pet odor) are natural refresh points after launch.

### Pinterest SEO notes

- **Pin title = the keyword.** Lead with the exact phrase people search ("probiotic cleaner vs enzyme cleaner," "why does pet odor come back after cleaning"), not a clever headline. Pinterest is a search engine first.
- **The first ~50 characters of the description carry the most weight** in search and in feed truncation — front-load the primary keyword and the reader's problem there, then let the rest of the description add context and the soft CTA.
- Also place the target keyword in: the board name and board description, the image text overlay (Pinterest reads overlay text), and the destination page title (already handled by article frontmatter).
- Use 3–8 relevant keyword phrases naturally per description; no hashtag walls, no keyword stuffing — 0–3 hashtags maximum, and only if they read naturally.
- Pin at a 2:3 ratio (1000×1500 px recommended); vertical, text-legible-at-thumbnail images outperform.
