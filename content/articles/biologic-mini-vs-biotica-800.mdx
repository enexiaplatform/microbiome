If you have narrowed your search to EnviroBiotics, the choice usually comes down to two devices: the **BioLogic Mini (Gen 2)** and the **Biotica 800**. Both release environmental probiotics into your space, both run on probiotic cartridges, and both carry the same manufacturer claims. What separates them is size, power, and where they live in your home.

**The 30-second answer:** choose the BioLogic Mini if you need to treat a small room or want a portable unit you can move around ($98, up to 300 sq ft). Choose the Biotica 800 if you want one device to cover your main living area ($299, up to 800 sq ft). Everything below explains why.

## Where they differ in practice

On paper these two look like the same idea at two sizes, and that is mostly true. The differences that matter show up in how you place them, how they are powered, and what upkeep looks like over a year.

### Coverage and placement

The BioLogic Mini is rated by the manufacturer for up to 300 sq ft per unit. That maps neatly onto a bedroom, a nursery, a home office, or the corner of a studio apartment where you and your pet actually spend time. Because it is small and self-contained, placement is simple: set it near the zone you care about, such as a pet bed or a crib, and let it run.

<ProductCard slug="biologic-mini" />

The Biotica 800 is rated for up to 800 sq ft per unit, which is a different job entirely. This is main-living-area territory: a living room, a combined kitchen-dining space, or a large primary suite. Rather than sitting next to one spot, it works as the single coverage point for the room the household gathers in.

One thing worth stating plainly: coverage figures describe the space a unit treats, not your whole floor plan. If your home has several separate rooms with doors that stay closed, no single unit of either size treats all of them at once.

### Power and portability

This is the biggest practical difference. The Mini is portable and battery-powered, so you can move it from room to room — desk during the day, nightstand at night. The manufacturer lists ultra-quiet operation among its features, which suits exactly those bedroom and nursery placements. There are also no filters to replace, only the probiotic cartridge.

The Biotica 800 is a plug-in design. The manufacturer offers it as wall-mount or freestanding, and its listed features include continuous, automated probiotic dispersal. In other words, it is built to be installed once and left alone — a set-it-and-forget-it appliance for a fixed space rather than a companion that follows you around.

If you know you want one device permanently handling the living room, plug-in is a feature, not a limitation. If your routine shifts between rooms, the Mini's battery is the whole point.

### Refill cadence and ongoing cost

Both devices run on probiotic cartridges the manufacturer rates at about the same interval: roughly every 90 days for the Mini, about every 3 months for the Biotica 800. Plan on around four cartridges per year for whichever you buy.

Upfront pricing is clear in our data: the Mini is $98 and the Biotica 800 is $299. Refill pricing is less settled. Our data notes Biotica 800 refill cartridges at about $59.99 from a reseller, but that figure is unverified, and we do not have a published refill price for the Mini. Check current cartridge pricing with the seller before committing, since refills are the real long-term cost of either device.

## Decision rules

Match the scenario to your home:

- **Studio apartment:** A Mini can cover your main zone if the space is compact and you spend most of your time in one area. If your studio runs larger than about 300 sq ft of actual living space, the Biotica 800 is the more honest fit.
- **Bedroom plus pet bed zone:** The Mini, placed near where your pet sleeps. This is the use case it was sized for, and the battery means no hunting for an outlet next to the dog bed.
- **Open-plan living room:** The Biotica 800. One unit, up to 800 sq ft, wall-mounted or freestanding, running continuously without you thinking about it.
- **Frequent mover between rooms:** The Mini. Portable and battery-powered means the device goes where you go instead of anchoring you to one outlet.
- **Multi-room coverage:** Do the math two ways. Two Minis ($196 total) let you treat two separate rooms at the same time, which suits homes with closed doors and distinct zones. One Biotica 800 ($299) concentrates up to 800 sq ft of coverage in a single connected space. Separate rooms favor the two-Mini logic; one big open space favors the Biotica.

## For pet owners specifically

Pet households tend to shop in this category for two reasons: dander and odor. Both are surface problems as much as air problems — dander settles into bedding, rugs, and the sofa your cat claims every afternoon, and organic odors linger where your pet sleeps.

For both devices, the manufacturer states the released environmental probiotics reduce common allergens — including dust mite allergen, pet dander, mold spores, and pollen — in the air and on surfaces, and reduce organic odors in the air and on surfaces. The manufacturer also states both are chemical-free, fragrance-free, and produce no ozone. Those are manufacturer claims, and we label them that way deliberately.

Here is the honest evidence picture: support for probiotic approaches in built environments is <EvidenceRating level="moderate" note="Evidence comes mainly from hospital-based studies of probiotic cleaning systems, not head-to-head home trials of these two devices." /> and comes mostly from hospital settings. A six-month hospital trial (Caselli et al., 2016) and a larger hospital study of a probiotic-based sanitation system (Caselli et al., 2018) found changes in surface microbial communities in clinical environments, and a 2025 review (Falagas et al.) maps the broader research landscape. A hospital ward is not a living room with a golden retriever, and there are no head-to-head home trials comparing the Mini and the Biotica 800 directly. We lay out exactly what this research can and cannot tell you in our explainer on [what environmental probiotics can and cannot do](/science/environmental-probiotics-what-they-can-cannot-do).

Practical takeaway for pet owners: place whichever unit you choose near your pet's primary sleeping area, keep up your normal cleaning and washing routines, and treat the device as a complement to — never a replacement for — ventilation, vacuuming, and vet guidance.

## FAQ

### Can the BioLogic Mini cover a whole apartment?

One Mini covers up to 300 sq ft, per the manufacturer, so it suits a single room or a compact studio's main zone. Larger or multi-room layouts need a second unit or a bigger model like the Biotica 800.

### How often do I need to replace refills?

Both devices use a probiotic cartridge the manufacturer rates at about 90 days (roughly 3 months). The Mini has no filters to replace; refill pricing for the Mini is not published in our data.

### Is the Biotica 800 worth three times the price of the Mini?

It depends on your space. At $299 versus $98, the Biotica 800 makes sense when you need one unit to cover a main living area up to 800 sq ft; for a bedroom or nursery, the Mini covers the job for less.

### Do these devices replace regular cleaning?

No. The manufacturer states the devices release environmental probiotics that reduce common allergens and organic odors in the air and on surfaces, but they are not a substitute for ordinary cleaning, ventilation, or veterinary care.

### Are these purifiers safe to run around pets?

The manufacturer states both devices are chemical-free, fragrance-free, and produce no ozone. As with any device, place it where curious pets cannot knock it over or chew cords.
