Intro paragraphs are plain markdown. **Bold**, *italic*, [editorial links](/about),
and `inline code` all work, as do GFM tables, lists, and blockquotes.

## ProductCard — single product showcase

Renders name, brand, price (or "See price"), key features, the first
manufacturer claim (labeled), and a clay CTA that routes through /go/<slug>.
Tracks product_card_view (IntersectionObserver, once) and affiliate_click.

<ProductCard slug="biologic-mini" />

Unverified products display their verification note instead of hiding it:

<ProductCard slug="purebiotics-pet" />

## ProductComparison — side-by-side table

Columns: price, coverage, format, refill cadence, best for. Comma-separated
slugs; unknown slugs are skipped. Tracks comparison_view once in view.

<ProductComparison slugs="biologic-mini,biotica-800,ba-2080" />

## ProsCons — balanced verdict panel

Pass arrays as JSX expressions:

<ProsCons
  pros={[
    "No filters to replace — just a probiotic cartridge",
    "Ultra-quiet; easy to move between rooms",
  ]}
  cons={[
    "Coverage tops out at 300 sq ft per unit",
    "Cartridge cost recurs about every 90 days",
  ]}
/>

## EvidenceRating — inline evidence badge

Levels: strong | moderate | limited. The optional note is exposed as a
tooltip and to screen readers.

The research here is <EvidenceRating level="moderate" note="Several small independent studies; larger trials still needed." /> and still developing.

## Callout — highlighted note box

<Callout type="info">
  EnviroBiotics rebranded from BetterAir in 2025 — older reviews may still use
  the BetterAir name.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
  Don't mix probiotic cleaners with disinfectants on the same surface at the
  same time; the disinfectant can knock out the beneficial bacteria.
</Callout>

## CTA — inline affiliate button

Always routes through /go/<slug> with source page and placement attached.
Placement is "inline" or "end". An end-of-article CTA is added automatically
for the first product in frontmatter; use inline CTAs inside the flow.

<CTA product="biotica-800" placement="inline">Check Biotica 800 pricing</CTA>

## Markdown basics that just work

- Lists, including task lists via GFM
- Tables (keep them narrow for mobile)
- > Blockquotes for pull-quotes
- Footnotes via GFM when you need them

Write calmly. Label claims. Cite sources. Never promise health outcomes.
