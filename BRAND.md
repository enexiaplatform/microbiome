**Decision: The Microbiome Home — themicrobiomehome.com**

**Rationale:**

- **Owns the category, not a product.** "Microbiome" is the search and cultural term the whole niche is converging on; the name lets us cover air purifiers, surface cleaners, and future verticals (gut, skin) without a rebrand.
- **Not tied to any merchant.** Unlike the legacy project (an EnviroBiotics-only affiliate hub), this name supports an independent, multi-brand editorial stance — which is the core quality gap in the current landscape.
- **"Home" frames the use case.** It signals environmental/home microbiome rather than supplement content, pre-filtering out the gut-health supplement audience we don't want in the MVP.
- **Trust register.** "The Microbiome Home" reads like a publication; "Biome-Friendly Home" reads like a product label and "PureBiome Living" reads like a supplement brand — both drift toward the spam-adjacent register we explicitly want to avoid.
- Domain was unregistered via RDAP as of 2026-07-19; secure it before any public announcement. Social handle availability: **UNVERIFIED**.

**Tagline:** *"Science-backed guides to a microbiome-friendly home — for you and your pets."*

## 2. Positioning Statement

> For US pet owners who want a fresher, lower-chemical home without falling for wellness hype, **The Microbiome Home** is the independent, science-first guide to environmental probiotic products — air purification and surface cleaning — that explains what the research actually shows, labels every manufacturer claim, and recommends products only with transparent affiliate disclosure.

**Differentiator:** the only content hub in this niche that is (a) multi-brand and independent, (b) built around the actual peer-reviewed evidence base with honest evidence ratings, and (c) explicit about the gap between hospital-study results and home use.

## 3. Audience

- **Who:** US pet owners, ages 28–55. Skews toward dog and cat households; includes parents of young children as a secondary overlap segment.
- **Mindset:** health-curious but hype-fatigued. They have tried enzyme cleaners, air purifiers, and "natural" products; odor and dander problems keep coming back; they distrust both bleach-everything culture and miracle-cure marketing.
- **Pain triggers:** recurring pet odor, dander/allergen worry, concern about harsh chemicals and synthetic fragrance around pets and kids, and conflicting advice from brand blogs and listicles.
- **Not our audience (MVP):** human gut-health/supplement shoppers, clinical/medical audiences, non-US markets.

## 4. Voice & Tone Rules

**Voice:** a calm, well-read friend who happens to read the actual studies. Editorial-magazine register, not influencer register.

**Rules:**

1. **Calm over hyped.** No exclamation-point marketing, no urgency tactics, no fear-baiting ("your home is making you sick").
2. **Attribute every product claim.** Anything a brand says about its product is phrased "The manufacturer states…" — never asserted in our editorial voice.
3. **Rate the evidence in the open.** Use the site's EvidenceRating levels (strong / moderate / limited) wherever research is cited, and say plainly when home-use evidence is thin.
4. **Plain language, real numbers.** Translate studies into everyday English, but keep citations, sample settings, and effect sizes visible. Never round "limited evidence" up to "proven."
5. **Balanced verdicts.** Every review includes real cons (price, refill cadence, coverage limits, evidence gaps). If we wouldn't say it to a skeptic, we don't publish it.
6. **Pet-owner empathy.** Write to the lived problem (the smell that comes back, the bedding you can't fully de-funk) before introducing any product.
7. **No medical voice.** We never diagnose, treat, or advise on health conditions. We point to veterinarians and physicians for that (see CLAIMS_POLICY.md).

**Tone sliders:** warm ↔ clinical → 70% warm. Cautious ↔ enthusiastic → 65% cautious. Plain ↔ technical → 80% plain.

## 6. What the Brand Is NOT

- **Not a supplement shop.** No gut-health pills, no probiotic supplements for humans or pets in the MVP. The name must never drift into supplement-spam territory.
- **Not clinical.** We are not a medical resource. No lab-coat aesthetic, no diagnostic language, no "treats allergies/asthma" framing — ever.
- **Not medical or veterinary advice.** We do not advise on health conditions for humans or animals. Standard pointer: "consult your veterinarian" / "consult your doctor." (Full policy: CLAIMS_POLICY.md.)
- **Not a brand mouthpiece.** No single-merchant focus (the explicit lesson from the legacy EnviroBiotics-only hub). Manufacturer claims are always labeled as such; cons are always published.
- **Not fear-based marketing.** We don't sell by scaring people about germs. The honest pitch is balance, not sterility — and we say plainly what these products can't do.
