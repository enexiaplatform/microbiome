| Wellness Mama | Generalist wellness publisher | Natural-living cleaning content | Specific articles covering this niche **UNVERIFIED**; broad wellness register, not pet-focused, not evidence-rated |

**Quality-gap analysis:**

1. **No independent, multi-brand hub.** Every specialist site is either a reseller or functionally single-brand. Nobody compares across merchants with disclosed incentives.
2. **Science is either absent or oversold.** Content either cites no research or implies hospital-study results apply directly to homes. Nobody shows an evidence rating.
3. **Pets are an afterthought.** Odor/dander content is generic; nothing is built around the pet owner's specific recurrence and safety concerns.
4. **"Best enzyme cleaner" listicles dominate commercial queries** — shallow, undisclosed or under-disclosed, and they conflate enzyme and probiotic products.

**Our position:** the science-first, pet-first, multi-brand hub that rates evidence honestly. The gap is the strategy.

## 5. Evidence Summary

All six sources are from **hospital or institutional settings**. None directly studies US homes or pets. This limitation is mandatory framing in every article that cites them (CLAIMS_POLICY.md §5).

| Study | One-line summary | Setting limitation |
|-------|------------------|--------------------|
| Vandini et al. 2014, PLoS ONE 9(9):e108598 | Early controlled study showing probiotic-based cleaning shifted hospital surface microbiota vs. conventional chemical cleaning. | Hospital surfaces; not homes. |
| Caselli et al. 2016, PLoS ONE 11(2):e0148857 | Probiotic hygiene system "remodulated" hospital surface microbiota and reduced resistance-gene carriage on surfaces. | Single-hospital setting; microbiological endpoints, not health outcomes. |
| Caselli et al. 2018, PLoS ONE 13(5):e0199616 | Across 6 hospitals, probiotic hygiene was associated with surface pathogens −83% on average vs. chlorine-based cleaning and HAI incidence falling from 4.8% to 2.3%. | Multi-hospital but non-randomized; institutional hygiene protocols don't transfer directly to homes. |
| Caselli et al. 2019, Infect Drug Resist 12:501–510 | Reported reduced antimicrobial-resistance markers and associated cost savings with probiotic hospital hygiene. | Hospital economics; no home applicability demonstrated. |
| Leistner et al. 2023, eClinicalMedicine 59:101958 | Cluster RCT comparing soap-based vs. disinfectant-based vs. probiotic cleaning regimens. | Institutional cluster trial; strongest design in the set, still not a home study. |
| Falagas et al. 2025, Biology 14(8):1043 | Review of probiotic cleaning evidence, summarizing promise and gaps. | Review article; reiterates that home-use evidence is limited. |

**Editorial bottom line:** evidence for environmental probiotic cleaning is *moderate in institutional settings* and *limited for home use*. Air-purification devices (e.g., the EnviroBiotics dispersal units) have thinner independent evidence than surface-cleaning protocols — surface evidence does **not** automatically extend to air dispersal. Default EvidenceRating for home-use claims: **limited**.

## 6. Claim-Risk Assessment

| Claim type | Risk | Disposition |
|------------|------|-------------|
| "Kills 99.9% of germs" / sterilization language | High | Banned. Rewrite: "is designed to reduce odor-causing bacteria on surfaces" (attributed to manufacturer where applicable) |
| "Relieves allergies" / "helps asthma" / any health-outcome claim | High | **Banned entirely** — no rewrite. Medical-claims territory |
| "Hospital studies prove it works in your home" | High | Banned. Mandatory hospital≠home framing |
| "Chemical-free, fragrance-free, no ozone" (device features) | Medium | Allowed only attributed: "The manufacturer states…" |
| "Green Seal certified" (PureBiotics unscented) | Low–Medium | Allowed attributed per manufacturer; independent verification **UNVERIFIED** |
| "Reduces pet dander / allergens in the air" | Medium | Allowed only as attributed manufacturer claim, with evidence rating |
| "Outperforms bleach/enzyme cleaners" | High | Banned as editorial assertion; hospital comparison data may be described with full setting caveats |
| "Vet recommended" / "pediatrician approved" | High | Banned — **UNVERIFIED** and health-adjacent |
| Price/coverage/refill specs | Low | Allowed from verified official sources; cite as-of date |

Full banned-phrase list and safe rewrites: CLAIMS_POLICY.md §4.
