## 1. Channel Strategy

One person, ~5–7 h/week. Not all platforms deserve equal effort.

| Platform | Role | Content type | Effort rank | Why |
|----------|------|--------------|-------------|-----|
| **Pinterest** | Search / evergreen traffic | Pins (2 per article), idea pins | **1** | Pins compound for months; audience matches (US homeowners, pet care, cleaning); lowest ongoing effort per click |
| **TikTok / Reels / Shorts** | Discovery | Short video from article scripts | **2** | One script serves three platforms; highest reach ceiling for a zero-follower account |
| **Instagram** | Community + link-in-bio | Carousels, Reels crosspost | **3** | Carousels repackage article structure well; grid is our visual storefront; link-in-bio routes to site articles |
| **Facebook** | Groups + page | Page posts, group answers | **4** | Pet-owner groups are high-intent but group participation doesn't scale; post page content, answer group questions only when genuinely helpful |
| **X / LinkedIn** | Authority | Threads, study breakdowns | **5** | Low buyer intent for this niche; post threads only because they're nearly free once the article exists |

**Decision: Pinterest + short video first.** Everything else is derivative output of those two workflows. Do not create net-new content for X, LinkedIn, or Facebook — only reformat.

**Profile basics (all platforms):** name "The Microbiome Home", tagline from BRAND.md §1, link = themicrobiomehome.com (or link-in-bio tool pointing to site articles only). Handle availability: **UNVERIFIED** (BRAND.md §1) — confirm before printing anywhere.

## 2. Content Engine — the Article Waterfall

Every blog article produces a fixed set of derivatives. One article in, six assets out. Never invent social-only claims; every derivative is a **compression** of the published article, not a new editorial product.

```
1 article ──┬─► 1 short-video script (45–60s) ──► TikTok + Reels + Shorts
            ├─► 1 carousel (6–8 slides) ─────────► Instagram (+ LinkedIn doc post)
            ├─► 2 static pins ───────────────────► Pinterest
            ├─► 1 thread (5–8 posts) ────────────► X
            └─► 1 discussion post ───────────────► Facebook page
```

**Transformation rules (apply to every derivative):**

1. **Hook = the article's pain point**, not the product. Start from the lived problem ("the dog-bed smell that survives the wash") before any solution. (BRAND.md §4.6.)
2. **Same claims, shorter sentences.** If a claim can't survive compression without losing its attribution or EvidenceRating, cut the claim. Never upgrade "limited evidence" to "works."
3. **Manufacturer claims stay attributed.** "The manufacturer states…" becomes "the brand says…" or on-screen text `[brand claim]`. Attribution must survive the format.
4. **Hospital ≠ home caveat travels.** Any video/carousel/thread citing the research base must include the one-line caveat: "These studies were in hospitals, not homes — home-use evidence is still limited." Non-negotiable (CLAIMS_POLICY.md §5).
5. **One CTA per asset, always to the site article.** "Full guide + sources at themicrobiomehome.com" — never to a raw affiliate URL (§6).
6. **Carousel structure:** slide 1 = hook, slides 2–6 = one article section each, slide 7 = honest caveat/con, final slide = CTA + disclosure if products are mentioned.
7. **Video structure:** 0–3s hook (the pain), 3–40s the explanation, 40–50s the honest limitation, 50–60s CTA to the article. No product in the first 3 seconds.
8. **Pins:** title = article's primary keyword phrased naturally; description = 1–2 sentences + keyword; both pins link to the same article with different visuals/hooks.

**Backlog order** (follows CONTENT_STRATEGY.md §1 publish order): science hub (#5) first for trust, then money pages (#1–#4), then spokes. Launch packs in `content/social/launch/` cover the first batch; the weekly cron (§7) works through the rest.

**Batching rule:** Tue–Thu are production blocks. If a week gets busy, protect Pinterest and video first (§1 ranking); skip X/FB without guilt.

## 4. Voice & Claims Rules for Social

CLAIMS_POLICY.md applies to every post, caption, comment, DM, and video script — character limits change nothing. Social-specific additions:

**Social-specific banned items (in addition to CLAIMS_POLICY.md §4):**

| Banned | Reason / safe rewrite |
|--------|----------------------|
| "Detox" (your home, your air, your dog's bed) | Fear-based, banned entirely (CLAIMS_POLICY.md §4) — no social exception |
| Health testimonials ("this fixed my allergies!") — ours or reposted | Health-outcome claim; never create, quote, or reshare |
| Before/after health framing (symptom → product → relief) | Implied health outcome; banned. Before/after **odor or visible mess** is allowed if it's our own real experience, framed as one household |
| "Vet approved / vet recommended" | Banned entirely (CLAIMS_POLICY.md §4) |
| "Chemical-free" as an editorial assertion | Attribute: "the brand says it's chemical-free" |
| Superlatives ("#1," "best on the market") without basis | "Our top pick for [use case]" with verifiable reasons |
| Fear hooks ("your home is making your dog sick") | Violates BRAND.md §4.1 — hook on the annoying problem, not danger |
| Comment-section health advice ("try this for your pet's condition") | Always: "we can't give health advice — your vet is the right call" |

**Comment/DM policy:** we answer product and cleaning questions from published content only. Anything health-adjacent gets the standard pointer (vet/doctor). Never argue with skeptics at length — one calm, sourced reply, then disengage. Skepticism is our audience (BRAND.md §3); treat it with respect.

**Voice check before posting:** would a calm, well-read friend say this? If it reads like an influencer or an ad, rewrite. (BRAND.md §4: 70% warm, 65% cautious, 80% plain.)

## 5. FTC & Platform Compliance

Baseline: FTC Endorsement Guides, 16 CFR Part 255 + CLAIMS_POLICY.md §6. Disclosure must be **unmissable in the format itself** — not buried in a hashtag cloud or behind "more."

| Platform | Disclosure format |
|----------|-------------------|
| Instagram | `#ad` or "affiliate link" in the **first line** of the caption (before the fold), any post mentioning a linked product. Use the platform's paid-partnership label if a brand relationship ever exists |
| TikTok | Verbal or on-screen "affiliate link in bio" within the video + `#ad` in caption |
| YouTube Shorts | "affiliate link" in description's first line + verbal/text mention in-video |
| Pinterest | "affiliate link" or `#ad` in pin description when the pin promotes a product-containing article |
| Facebook | Plain sentence at the top of the post: "This post contains affiliate links — we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you." |
| X | `#ad` or "(affiliate link)" in any post that links a product-bearing page |

**Rules that don't bend:**

1. **Link-in-bio → site articles only.** The bio link always routes to a themicrobiomehome.com article (which carries the auto-rendered disclosure box). Never link a raw affiliate or merchant URL from any social surface.
2. **Amazon Associates:** never place Amazon links in DMs, email, or closed/private groups — Amazon's Operating Agreement prohibits it, and we route through site articles anyway. Any Amazon-linked content carries the exact line *"As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases."*
3. **Free samples / sponsored anything:** disclosed in the content itself if ever accepted (CLAIMS_POLICY.md §6.5). At launch: none accepted.
4. **No dark patterns:** no "link in comments" games to dodge disclosure, no disguising affiliate posts as pure editorial.

## 6. Link & Tracking Strategy

**Golden rule: social always links to site articles, never to raw affiliate URLs.** The article carries the disclosure, the evidence framing, and the `/go/<slug>` redirect layer (AFFILIATE_LINKS.md). This keeps every click FTC-clean and keeps affiliate URLs server-side only.

**How `/go/<slug>` works for social campaigns** (mechanics: AFFILIATE_LINKS.md):

- Articles render affiliate links as `/go/<slug>?source=<page-path>&placement=<placement>`.
- The redirect copies only `utm_*` and `subid` params onto the merchant destination; `source`/`placement` are logged, not forwarded.
- For social attribution, append params to the **article link** (not the `/go` link — that's generated by the site):

```
https://themicrobiomehome.com/articles/why-pet-odor-returns-after-cleaning?utm_source=pinterest&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=launch
```

**UTM convention (fixed, no improvisation):**

| Param | Values |
|-------|--------|
| `utm_source` | `pinterest` · `tiktok` · `instagram` · `facebook` · `twitter` · `youtube` · `linkedin` |
| `utm_medium` | `social` (always) |
| `utm_campaign` | `launch` (launch-pack content) · `weekly` (cron-batch content) · `evergreen` (re-pinned older articles) |

- Site-side analytics reads these on the landing page (ANALYTICS_SETUP.md); affiliate clicks after arrival are logged with `source` = article path.
- If a future campaign needs per-post granularity, add `subid=<platform>-<yyyymmdd>-<slug>` to the article URL — it passes through `/go` to the merchant when affiliate programs go live. Note: all affiliate program terms are currently **UNVERIFIED** (AFFILIATE_LINKS.md §Verification status) — env vars stay empty until confirmed.

## 7. The Weekly Automation

A scheduled cron task (Kimi Work Automation) runs weekly and generates a fresh batch file:

```
content/social/weekly/YYYY-MM-DD.md
```

**What the batch contains:** the next article(s) from the waterfall backlog (§2 order), each expanded into the full derivative set — 1 video script, 1 carousel outline, 2 pin titles/descriptions, 1 X thread, 1 FB post — all pre-checked against CLAIMS_POLICY.md and pre-tagged with UTM links per §6.

**How the owner uses it (fits the §3 cadence):**

1. **Monday, 15 min:** open the latest `weekly/YYYY-MM-DD.md`, review for voice and claims, edit anything that reads like an ad. The batch is a draft; the owner is the publisher.
2. **Paste into free native schedulers** — no paid tools:
   - **Pinterest:** native pin scheduler (business account)
   - **Instagram/Facebook:** Meta Business Suite
   - **TikTok:** TikTok Studio (web)
   - **X/LinkedIn:** post directly, ~10 min
3. Mark the batch file done (rename or check off in-file) so next week's cron moves to the next backlog items.

Launch-period content (first 2–4 weeks) comes from the pre-built packs in `content/social/launch/`; the cron takes over once those are exhausted.

## 8. Metrics & Monthly Review Loop

**Watch per platform (weekly glance, monthly review):**

| Platform | Primary metric | Secondary | Ignore |
|----------|---------------|-----------|--------|
| Pinterest | Outbound clicks to site | Impressions trend, saves | Follower count |
| TikTok/Reels/Shorts | Profile/link clicks | Watch completion, shares | Likes |
| Instagram | Link-in-bio taps | Saves, carousel completion | Follower count |
| Facebook | Post clicks | Group-answer profile visits | Page likes |
| X/LinkedIn | Link clicks | Profile visits | Impressions vanity |
| Site (GA) | `utm_medium=social` sessions → article scroll depth → `affiliate_click` events | — | — |

**Monthly review loop (last Friday, 30 min):**

1. Pull site analytics: which `utm_source` drove sessions and affiliate clicks?
2. **Double down:** any platform or content type in the top 2 for outbound clicks gets more slots next month.
3. **Stop:** any format with <1% click-through after 8+ posts gets cut or reworked — starting with the §1 lowest-ranked platforms.
4. **Refresh winners:** top 3 pins/videos get new versions pointing at the same articles (Pinterest especially rewards fresh creatives on proven URLs).
5. Note findings at the bottom of that month's latest `weekly/` batch file; feed conclusions into the quarterly content review (CONTENT_STRATEGY.md §4).

**North star:** social exists to send problem-aware pet owners to site articles. Engagement that doesn't move site sessions or affiliate clicks is decoration.
